Lake Worth Beach is one of Palm Beach County's most eclectic cities — a walkable, arts-oriented community with a rich stock of historic homes from the early and mid-20th century. The bungalows, Craftsman cottages, and Mediterranean Revival homes near the historic downtown require painting approaches that respect original construction: careful prep around original wood trim and moldings, and color choices that suit the historic character of the neighborhood. Scollo Painting has experience with all of these considerations.

Lake Worth Beach's location on the Lake Worth Lagoon and proximity to the Atlantic means coastal conditions apply throughout the city. Exterior projects benefit from thorough surface cleaning that removes salt deposits and biological growth, and from coatings selected for salt-air resistance and UV stability. The older housing stock also presents interior challenges — hairline cracking from decades of seasonal movement, and moisture-related damage near bathrooms and kitchens that needs proper attention before the new finish goes on.

Frequently asked questions about painting in Lake Worth, FL

How much does painting cost in Lake Worth Beach, FL?

Exterior painting in Lake Worth Beach typically costs $3,200–$8,500, depending on home size, condition, and coating. The city's housing stock ranges from smaller historic cottages near the beach to larger homes in northern neighborhoods — pricing reflects the specific scope and prep requirements of each property. Interior work is quoted separately based on room count and ceiling height.

Can you paint historic homes in the Lake Worth Beach historic district?

Yes — we're experienced working with Lake Worth Beach's historic properties, including homes in the historic district near downtown and the older neighborhoods east of I-95. Historic homes often have wood siding, older stucco, and architectural details that require specific prep approaches and careful hand-painting to preserve their character.

Do you offer painting for Lake Worth Beach rental properties?

Yes — we regularly work with rental property owners in Lake Worth Beach, including single-family rentals, duplexes, and small apartment buildings. We understand turnaround requirements and can schedule efficiently between tenants. We also use durable, washable interior finishes that hold up to tenant use and make subsequent touch-up work faster and easier.

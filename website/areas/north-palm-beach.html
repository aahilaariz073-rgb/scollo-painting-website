Painting FAQs for North Palm Beach residents

Q: How does living on a canal or the Intracoastal affect paint longevity in North Palm Beach?

A: Waterfront homes in North Palm Beach — whether on a canal or directly on the Intracoastal — experience significantly accelerated paint film degradation compared to homes a mile or two inland. Salt air and wind-driven moisture are the primary culprits. We use premium 100% acrylic or elastomeric exterior coatings with corrosion-inhibiting primers on any ferrous metal surfaces, and recommend a recoating inspection every 5 years for Intracoastal-facing elevations.

Q: What's the typical cost of exterior painting for a ranch-style North Palm Beach home?

A: A classic North Palm Beach ranch-style home of 1,500–2,200 sq ft typically runs $3,000 to $5,500 for a complete exterior repaint including pressure washing, surface prep, and two finish coats of premium paint. Homes with extensive pool decks, screened enclosures, or ornate trim work may run higher. We provide free written estimates for every project.

Q: Do you paint condominiums at Old Port Cove in North Palm Beach?

A: Yes — we serve multi-unit residential buildings and condominiums throughout North Palm Beach, including Old Port Cove. We work with condo association boards and property managers to plan phased painting projects that minimize disruption to residents, and we carry the insurance and licensing documentation condo associations typically require before approving contractor access.

Q: Can you schedule interior painting for when I'm returning to North Palm Beach for the season?

A: Absolutely. Many North Palm Beach homeowners are seasonal residents who want their interiors freshened up before they arrive or after they leave. We coordinate advance scheduling for seasonal residents, work around your arrival dates, and keep your home secure and in order throughout the project. Call early before the season starts to lock in your slot.

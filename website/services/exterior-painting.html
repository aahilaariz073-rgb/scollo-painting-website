Exterior Painting

Exterior painting that stands up to Florida's climate.

Florida's combination of intense UV, humidity, salt air, and heavy summer rains is brutal on exterior paint. Scollo Painting has been protecting homes and buildings across the Treasure Coast for over 45 years — with the prep work, the right coatings, and the experience to make a finish last. We cover siding, stucco, trim, doors, fences, and more across Stuart and Palm Beach County.

What's included

Preparation is everything in Florida's exterior environment.

In most climates, surface prep accounts for maybe half the outcome of an exterior paint job. In South Florida, it accounts for nearly all of it. Algae, mildew, chalky oxidized paint, moisture-compromised stucco, and salt-contaminated surfaces all need specific treatment before a new coating goes on — otherwise the new paint will fail just as fast as whatever it replaced. That's not an opinion; it's just chemistry in a subtropical climate.

Every exterior project we take on begins with a thorough assessment of the surface. We identify areas where old paint is peeling or bubbling, where caulking has cracked or failed, where stucco has begun to separate, and where moisture may have already gotten behind the surface. We address all of those issues before we open a can of paint.

Our exterior painting process

Power washing. We start by thoroughly power washing the entire exterior — removing algae, mildew, dirt, loose paint, and chalking from every surface. On stucco homes, this step is especially important: a surface with embedded mildew or efflorescence will cause new paint to fail quickly regardless of paint quality.

Caulking, patching & priming. Once the surface is clean and dry, we re-caulk all joints, seams, and gaps around windows, doors, and trim. Cracked stucco is repaired with appropriate patching compound and allowed to fully cure. We prime bare or patched areas with exterior-grade primer suited to the substrate — critical for adhesion and long-term durability.

Florida-grade coatings. We select exterior paints designed for high UV exposure, high humidity, and wind-driven rain — products that include mildewcide, resist fading, and maintain flexibility through Florida's temperature swings. We're not applying budget coatings; we're applying the products we'd use on our own homes.

Stucco expertise. The vast majority of homes in Palm Beach and Martin County are stucco construction. We know how to paint stucco properly — including the specific primer requirements, the right application thickness, how to handle existing elastomeric coatings, and what to look for when stucco has absorbed moisture. It's a substrate that punishes shortcuts, and we don't take them.

Trim, doors & accent surfaces. We paint wood and metal trim, exterior doors, shutters, garage doors, fences, and railings. Each surface type gets the specific prep and finish it needs — sanded trim, rust-treated metal, properly bonded paint on smooth door surfaces.

Power Wash & Prep

Every job starts with a thorough power wash to remove algae, mildew, chalking, and loose paint. Proper prep is the single biggest factor in how long exterior paint lasts in Florida's climate.

Florida-Grade Coatings

We use premium exterior paints formulated for UV resistance, humidity tolerance, and mildew inhibition — coatings that hold color and adhesion through South Florida's harsh conditions, not just the first season.

Stucco Expertise

Most homes on the Treasure Coast are stucco construction — and stucco painted incorrectly fails fast. We know stucco: proper primers, application methods, moisture assessment, and elastomeric coating compatibility.

Weather Monitoring

Paint applied in the wrong conditions — high humidity, threat of rain, temperature extremes — fails prematurely. We monitor conditions and schedule work to ensure optimal application every time.

Why Scollo

Why our exterior work outlasts the competition

South Florida's climate puts exterior paint through conditions that would destroy a poorly prepared job within a year or two. The UV index here is among the highest in the continental United States — it breaks down paint binders and fades pigments far faster than in northern states. The rainy season brings weeks of daily downpours that probe every crack and improperly caulked seam. Salt air from the ocean works its way into surfaces and creates adhesion problems for paint that wasn't applied over the right primer. We've watched all of these failure modes up close for over 45 years, and our process is designed around preventing every one of them.

We don't cut corners on dry time, we don't apply paint when conditions are marginal, and we don't use lower-grade products to pad a margin. What we do is take the time to do the job the way it needs to be done — and give you a fixed price up front so there are no surprises at the end. The result is an exterior finish that looks great when we leave and still looks great years later.

Whether it's a single-family home, a townhouse, a condo building, or a commercial property, the same standard applies: thorough prep, the right products, and clean execution from a team that's been doing this work in South Florida longer than most painting companies in the area have existed.
